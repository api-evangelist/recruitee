Most organizations treat recruitment as a response to urgency. A role opens, a job post goes live, and the clock starts ticking. It works — until it doesn't. Fewer than 25% of leaders believe their organization consistently makes high-quality people decisions — including hiring decisions. Not because people aren't trying, but because recruitment is often disconnected from the organizational goals it's supposed to serve. Strategic recruitment changes that. It's not a checklist of best practices. It's the structural shift from filling vacancies to making better decisions about people — decisions that affect performance, retention, and growth long after someone starts.
